# Emoji Invaders – Play Guide

## Introduction

Emoji Invaders is an arcade-style shooter inspired by classic games like Space Invaders. The objective is simple: survive as long as possible, destroy enemy waves, and achieve the highest score you can.

At its core, the game follows the traditional formula, but it also adds power-ups, boss fights, and difficulty scaling to keep things interesting. The mechanics are easy to understand, but getting consistent high scores takes some practice.



## Core Objective

Your main goal is to:

* Destroy all enemies in each wave
* Avoid being hit by enemy bullets
* Prevent enemies from reaching or colliding with you
* Survive through multiple rounds, including boss encounters

Each wave gets progressively harder, so staying calm and consistent matters more than playing aggressively all the time.



## Basic Controls

* `A`: Move left
* `D`: Move right
* `Space`: Shoot
* `Escape`: Return to menu

Movement is simple, but positioning is everything. Small adjustments often make the difference.



## Enemy Formation and Behavior

Enemies are arranged in rows and move together across the screen. When they hit the edge, they reverse direction and move closer to the player.

### Key Mechanics

* As enemies are destroyed, the remaining ones move faster
* Lower enemies are more dangerous because they are closer to you
* Enemy shooting behavior depends on their importance level

### Enemy Types

* **Easy enemies (importance 1)**

  * Low threat
  * Rarely shoot

* **Medium enemies (importance 2)**

  * Moderate threat
  * Shoot occasionally

* **Hard enemies (importance 3)**

  * Higher threat
  * Shoot more frequently

In practice, you will often find yourself prioritizing certain enemies even if they are not the closest ones.



## Shooting and Combat

You fire upward and need to time your shots carefully.

* Enemy bullets travel downward and must be avoided
* Shooting constantly can help, but positioning matters more
* Shields can block shots, but they do not last forever

As the number of enemies decreases, the pace increases, which is where most mistakes tend to happen.



## Shields

Shields are placed between you and the enemies.

* They block both enemy and player bullets
* Once destroyed, they are gone until restored
* They give you temporary protection, but should not be relied on completely

It is usually better to use shields as a backup rather than your main strategy.



## Power-Ups

Power-ups drop from enemies and give temporary advantages.

### Types

* **Rapid fire (bolt)**

  * Greatly reduces shooting delay

* **Speed boost (fast)**

  * Increases player movement speed

* **Shield restore (shield)**

  * Recreates the shield line

Most power-ups are time-limited, so using them at the right moment can make a big difference.



## UFO Encounters

A UFO may occasionally appear at the top of the screen.

* Moves independently from the main formation
* Harder to hit due to unpredictable movement
* Provides higher score rewards and better drop chances

Going for the UFO is a bit of a risk, but it can pay off if timed well.



## Boss Rounds

Every few waves, a boss replaces the normal enemy formation.

### Boss Characteristics

* Requires multiple hits to defeat
* Fires more frequently than regular enemies
* Moves independently from standard patterns
* Can spawn power-ups during the fight

Boss fights are less about rushing damage and more about staying alive long enough to finish the fight.



## Scoring System

Points are awarded for destroying enemies:

* Easy enemies -> lower score
* Medium enemies -> moderate score
* Hard enemies -> higher score
* UFO and boss -> highest rewards

Your goal is to maximize score while staying alive for as long as possible.



## Difficulty Scaling

The game becomes harder over time:

* Enemy movement speed increases as enemies are eliminated
* Shooting frequency increases within a wave
* Boss fights introduce sudden difficulty spikes

Later waves can feel quite intense, especially when only a few fast enemies remain.



## Strategy Tips

* Reduce enemy numbers early to control the pace
* Prioritize enemies that shoot more often
* Use shields when needed, but do not depend on them
* Save power-ups for moments when things start getting out of control
* Stay mobile, especially in later waves
* During boss fights, focus on survival first and damage second

If something goes wrong, it is usually because of positioning rather than reaction time.



## Personal Note

This project started as a simple recreation of a classic arcade game, but it gradually became a way to experiment with different mechanics like power-ups, bosses, and difficulty scaling.

Some parts are still being refined, especially balancing, but that was also one of the most interesting aspects to work on. Small changes in speed or shooting behavior can completely change how the game feels.
